Are you curious about the future of jobs, especially in IT and cybersecurity? With technology evolving every day, it can be pretty confusing. But don't worry! We're here to break it down for you, so it's easy to understand.

# **What's Happening with Jobs and Technology?**

- Layoffs in Tech: Many big tech companies have been letting go of employees. This might sound scary, but it's not all doom and gloom!

- The Economy and AI: Changes in the economy and advancements in AI are affecting jobs, but not every industry will be impacted in the same way.

- Shifting Job Landscapes: New technology can change which jobs are available, but it also creates new opportunities!

Even with layoffs at big companies, there's still plenty of hope for finding a job in IT or cybersecurity. Not everyone who loses a job at a company like Google will want (or need) to jump into another tech job right away.

# **The State of Cybersecurity Jobs**

Despite the challenges, the cybersecurity job market is looking promising!

- There's a 72% supply-demand ratio for cybersecurity jobs.

- Historical data shows the number of cybersecurity jobs has been growing since 2010.

- The Bureau of Labor Statistics predicts a 32% increase in cybersecurity jobs from 2022 to 2032.

If you're considering a career in cybersecurity, it's a field with lots of opportunities!

# **How to Prepare for Your Future in Cybersecurity**

Wondering how to get ready for a career in cybersecurity? Focus on learning and improving your skills:

- Gain the necessary skills for cybersecurity jobs.

- Earn certifications that are in demand.

- Develop yourself in areas where you're not as strong.

Studying hard and staying current with the latest technology trends are great ways to prepare for a successful career in cybersecurity or any tech field!

# **The Value of a Cybersecurity Degree in 2024**

Is getting a cybersecurity degree worth it? Absolutely!

- It's filled with marketable certifications and has the NSA's stamp of approval.

- It can help you work in defense and government, providing job security and good pay.

- You can complete a cybersecurity degree relatively quickly and affordably, especially if you plan ahead.

If you love solving problems and are interested in technology, a degree in cybersecurity could be a great choice for you!

Even though the job market and technology keep changing, there are many reasons to be optimistic about the future, especially in cybersecurity.
