## **Is It Possible to Start with Zero Experience?**

First off, yes, it's totally possible to start learning cyber security from scratch. You don't need a background in IT to begin. However, while it's doable, it might not be the easiest or quickest path to take. But don't worry! I'm here to guide you through it.

## **What You Need to Know**

Getting a job in cyber security, or anywhere in tech, comes down to two main steps:

- Getting an interview
- Passing an interview

Seems simple, right? But here's the catch: in cyber security, experience matters a lot. So, if you're starting with zero IT or cyber security experience, you'll need to make sure other parts of your resume are extra strong. ## **A Quicker Alternative**

While aiming directly for a cyber security job is admirable, there's a faster way to enter the tech world:

- Start with a more accessible IT job, such as a help desk position. This could take around 3 months with focused studying.
- Every IT job has elements of cyber security. Use this as a learning opportunity to sharpen your skills.
- Work on projects that showcase your security-focused abilities, like creating incident response playbooks or managing user access securely.
- Keep applying for both IT and cyber security jobs, not just ones with "cyber" in the title.

This approach not only gets you working and earning faster but also helps you gain valuable IT experience that's applicable to cyber security.

## **In Conclusion**

Finding your way into a cyber security career without an IT background might seem daunting, but with the right strategy, it's entirely achievable.
