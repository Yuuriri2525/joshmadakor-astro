Picture this, building a career in cyber security and earning relevant industry-recognized certifications, all at an affordable rate. Sounds too good to be true? Well, not anymore! Read on to discover a fantastic cybersecurity program backed by the National Security Agency (NSA).

## **Program Overview**

The NSA, along with several other Federal Partners, run a special program known as the National Centers of Academic Excellence in Cyber Security (CAE). The goal? To set standards for cyber security curriculum in colleges across the nation, making sure students are learning up-to-date, relevant information. If colleges meet these standards, they can apply to become a designated CAE, a great plus point for students when seeking impressive jobs like a Navy Cyber Warfare Engineer.

## **College Highlight: Western Governors University**

Western Governors University (WGU) is honored to be recognized as a CAE by the NSA. What's great about WGU? It's an online accredited university with an array of flexible programs that can be completed at your pace, for a budget-friendly fee.

- WGU’s Cyber Security and Information Assurance course is crafted under the newest DHS (Department of Homeland Security) and NSA guidelines.

## **WGU's Unique Approach**

WGU offers a unique learning model by focusing on results rather than time spent in a classroom. This model, called competency-based education, allows students to move through their course at their own speed. Once you master the course material, you move on to the next class. And guess what? There's no homework to turn in! To pass each class, you either have to submit a series of papers, take a multiple-choice exam, or a combination of both.

## **Planning Towards a Degree**

Here’s a basic outline that help you plan out your WGU journey:

1. Speak with an enrollment counselor at WGU regarding your course interest and any existing college credits to be transferred.
2. Utilize platforms like [Study.com](https://study.com/?adkey=bbadc86ebcb6462c85cbe3e02528ec34&_channel=ambassador&_campaign=blog) to find, take, and transfer credits into the program. This can significantly cut down the overall cost.
3. Take an overview of the remaining classes at WGU. Then consider "pre-studying" for classes that require certifications to pass.
4. Finish enrolling at WGU and complete the rest of the classes in one or two terms. Voila, you have a bachelor's degree in Cyber Security and Information Assurance from a CAE at an affordable price!

Once you graduate with your degree and all those impressive certifications, what's next? Time to dive into the job market! Here's a glimpse at the job prospects at various career stages:

- **Entry-level:** Cyber Security Analyst, Systems Administrator, IT Auditor
- **Mid-career:** Information Security Analyst, IT Project Manager, Security Consultant
- **Late-career:** Chief Information Security Officer, Information Security Director, IT Director
