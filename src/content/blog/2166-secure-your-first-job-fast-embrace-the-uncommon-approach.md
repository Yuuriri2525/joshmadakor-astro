## **The Fast-Track Strategy**

Our gold-level strategy might be a bit of a surprise. But here it is, expand the geographic region you're willing to work in. This strategy works wonders, especially when you're new to the field and you're trying to break into the industry from scratch. Landing your maiden job could be a little tough, so we need to be resourceful, don't we?

## **How It Works**

- **Faster Experience Gains:** By extending your job search geography, you'll likely find a job sooner and start getting valuable work experience faster than you would if you only looked for jobs in your local area.

## **Overcoming the Challenges**

Okay, moving can be a pretty big undertaking. It could even cost you some cash. But hang on, there's actually good news here. Believe it or not, the economics behind this strategy can actually work out quite favorably in your favor. Wondering how? Let's take a look.

- **More Chances:** By increasing your job application rate and extending your job hunt geography, you're more likely to land a job quicker than if you only job-hunted in your local area.
- **Less Competition:** If you choose to apply to areas that aren't prominent Tech hubs but still have tech jobs, competition will be much lower than in bigger cities, enhancing your chances of success.
- **Opportunity Cost Benefits:** This simply means the money you save by doing one thing over another. For example, if you could find a job in another region sooner than in your hometown, the salary you earn during this period would offset any moving costs and actually put you at a financial advantage.
