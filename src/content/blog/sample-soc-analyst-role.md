The Security Operations Center (SOC) is where most people start their security career. It's a
great first seat: you see real incidents every day and learn fast.

## What the job is really like

You monitor alerts, investigate the suspicious ones, and escalate the real threats. The work is
equal parts pattern recognition and clear writing.

### A typical shift

- Review the alert queue and triage by severity
- Pull logs and piece together what happened
- Document findings so the next analyst can pick up where you left off

## How to get hired

1. Get comfortable with a SIEM (Sentinel, Splunk, or similar)
2. Learn the basics of networking and Windows event logs
3. Build a portfolio of investigations you can walk through

> Interviewers don't expect you to know everything — they want to see how you *think* through a
> problem you've never seen before.

## Keep growing

Once you're in, specialize: threat hunting, detection engineering, or incident response. The SOC
is the door, not the destination.
